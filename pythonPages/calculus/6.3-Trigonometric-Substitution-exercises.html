6.3 Exercises — Trigonometric Substitution

For each of exercises 1–3, use a trigonometric substitution to remove the root sign.

\(1.\) \(\sqrt{36 - x^2}\) \(2.\) \(\sqrt{100 + 9x^2}\) \(3.\) \(\sqrt{25x^2 - 144}\)

For each of exercises 4–14, evaluate the integral using a trigonometric substitution.

\(4.\) \(\ds \int \sqrt{9 - x^2} \di x\) \(5.\) \(\ds \int_0^{1/2} \frac{x^2}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \di x \) \(6.\) \(\ds \int_{-1/2}^{-1/4} \frac{\sqrt{16x^2 - 1}}{2x} \di x\)
\(7.\) \(\ds \int \frac{1}{x \sqrt{x^2 - 25}} \di x\) \(8.\) \(\ds \int_{3 \sqrt 3 /2}^{3/2} \frac{1}{x \sqrt{4x^2 + 9}} \di x\) \(9.\) \(\ds \int \frac{x^5}{\sqrt{4 - x^2}} \di x\)
\(10.\) \(\ds \int_{4/ \sqrt 3}^4 \frac{x^3}{\sqrt{x^2 - 4}} \di x\) \(11.\) \(\ds \int \frac{x^3}{\sqrt{4 + x^2}} \di x \) \(12.\) \(\ds \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2 - 4x + 1}} \di x \)
\(13.\) \(\ds \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{13 - 2x - x^2}} \di x\) \(14.\) \(\ds \int \frac{x^2}{(4 - 9x^2)^{3/2}} \di x\)
\(15.\)
Show that the area bounded by ellipse \(x^2/a^2 + y^2/b^2 = 1\) is \(\pi ab.\)
