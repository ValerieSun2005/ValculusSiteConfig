3.5 Exercises — Curve Sketching

Click on a problem number to see its solution.

For each of exercises 1–9, find the derivative using logarithmic differentiation.

\(1.\) \(\ds y = e^x \cos x\) \(2.\) \(\ds y = [\sin 3x]^{x + 7}\) \(3.\) \(\ds y = x^4 \cos(9^x)\)
\(4.\) \(\ds y = x^4 \cos(9^x)\) \(5.\) \(\ds y = \frac{x \sin x}{\cos(x^3)}\) \(6.\) \(\ds y = x^x\)
\(7.\) \(\ds y = \frac{3 - x}{5^x \tan x} \) \(8.\) \(\ds y = \frac{\sec^2 x}{x^3 + \ln x}\) \(9.\) \(\ds y = \frac{e^x}{x \cos(x^2 - 1)}\)
\(10.\)
Consider the implicit equation given by {fig} \[y^2 \sin y = \cos(x^2) e^x \period \] Using logarithmic differentiation, find \(\deriv{y}{x}\) in terms of \(x\) and \(y.\)
\(11.\)
Consider the implicit equation given by {fig} \[y^2 \sin y = \cos(x^2) e^x \period \] Using logarithmic differentiation, find \(\deriv{y}{x}\) in terms of \(x\) and \(y.\)
\(12.\)
Consider the equation given by {fig} \[y^2 \sin y = \cos(x^2) e^x \period \] Using logarithmic differentiation, find \(\deriv{y}{x}\) in terms of \(x\) and \(y.\)
\(13.\)
Consider the implicit equation given by \[3^y \cos(y^2 - 4) = x^3 + \log_4 x \period\] Using tt differentiation, find \(\deriv{y}{x}\) in terms of \(x\) and \(y.\)
\(14.\)
Consider the implicit equation given by \[3^y \cos(y^2 - 4) = x^3 + \log_4 x \period\] Using tt differentiation, find \(\deriv{y}{x}\) in terms of \(x\) and \(y.\)
