10.3 Exercises — Infinite Geometric Series

Click on a problem number to see its solution.

For each of exercises 1–10, evaluate the infinite geometric series or show that it diverges.

\(1.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n \) \(2.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^n \)
\(3.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{3}{5^n} \) \(4.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{5^n}{e^{n + 1}} \)
\(5.\) \(\displaystyle 3 - \frac{3}{5} + \frac{3}{25} - \frac{3}{125} + \cdots \) \(6.\) \(\displaystyle 2 \pi + 2 \pi^2 + 2 \pi^3 + 2 \pi^4 + \cdots \)
\(7.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{e^{n + 3}}{\pi^n} \) \(8.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{2^{3n - 1}}{7^n} \)
\(9.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{5^{2n}}{e^{n + 1}} \) \(10.\) \(\displaystyle \frac{2}{3} - \frac{2 e}{9} + \frac{2 e^2}{27} - \frac{2e^3}{81} + \cdots \)

For each of exercises 11–16, determine the values of \(k\) for which the geometric series converges.

\(11.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \left(\frac{2k}{3}\right)^n \) \(12.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{k^n}{3^{n + 2}} \) \(13.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \left(k^2 - 6k + 10\right)^n \)
\(14.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \left(\frac{2k}{k - 8}\right)^n \) \(15.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \left(\frac{2}{k^2 - 8k + 16}\right)^n \) \(16.\) \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 10}^\infty \left(\frac{k}{2k + 3}\right)^n \)

For each of exercises 17–22, express the repeating decimal as a fraction.

\(17.\) \(\displaystyle 0.387\overline{2} \) \(18.\) \(\displaystyle 0.\overline{4} \) \(19.\) \(\displaystyle 5.3\overline{2} \)
\(20.\) \(\displaystyle 6.23\overline{1} \) \(21.\) \(\displaystyle 2.8\overline{5} \) \(22.\) \(\displaystyle 4.6\overline{7} \)
\(23.\)
A bouncy ball is dropped from \(100\) feet. Each time the ball hits the floor, it rebounds to one-third of the height from which it fell. What is the total vertical distance the ball travels?
\(24.\)
Suppose that the citizens of a country, on average, spend \(70\) percent of their income and save \(30\) percent. Because money changes hands indefinitely, the government can spend \(x\) dollars to generate \[x + 0.7x + (0.7)^2 x + (0.7)^3 x + \cdots\] dollars of economic activity. How much money must the government spend to generate \(\$60\) billion of economic activity?
