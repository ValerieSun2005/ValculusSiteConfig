Position, Velocity, and Acceleration

Consider an object traveling in a straight line, modeled by the \(x\)-axis. The object's position is its location from \(x = 0.\) For example, an object that travels \(3\) units to the right of \(x = 0\) has position \(x = 3.\) An object's velocity is its speed in a certain direction: If the object travels toward the positive direction of the \(x\)-axis, then its velocity is positive; if the object travels toward the negative direction, then its velocity is negative. Velocity is the rate at which position changes with respect to time.
